## Overview of the Approach

Gary Habermas developed the Minimal Facts methodology to study the resurrection of Jesus by focusing on events that are strongly supported by historical evidence and recognized by the vast majority of scholars, even those skeptical of supernatural claims. Unlike traditional apologetic methods that rely on the reliability of the New Testament as a whole, this approach emphasizes facts that are virtually undeniable based on multiple independent lines of evidence. The goal is to establish a foundation of historical certainty before drawing conclusions. 

## Criteria for Minimal Facts

Habermas sets two main requirements for an event to qualify as a Minimal Fact:
1. Strong evidential support: Each fact must be confirmed by several independent and critically assessed arguments, ensuring its historical credibility. 
2. Scholarly consensus: The fact must be acknowledged as historical by the majority of contemporary scholars in relevant fields, including liberal, skeptical, or atheist scholars. 

The first criterion is considered the most crucial, as it directly establishes the historicity of the event, while the second demonstrates broad scholarly agreement. 


## Core Minimal Facts

Habermas typically identifies six key facts about Jesus' death and resurrection that meet these criteria:

- **Jesus died by crucifixion** under Roman authority, confirmed by multiple sources including the Gospels, Paul's letters, and non-Christian historians like Josephus and Tacitus.
- **Post-death appearances:** Soon after his death, Jesus' followers experienced appearances they believed were of the risen Jesus.
- **Transformation of disciples:** The disciples' lives were radically changed, demonstrating bold proclamation of the resurrection, even to the point of martyrdom.
- **Early proclamation:** The resurrection message was taught very early, within a few years of Jesus' death.
- **Conversion of James:** Jesus' brother James, initially skeptical, became a believer after experiencing what he interpreted as the risen Jesus.
- **Conversion of Paul:** Former persecutor Paul (Saul of Tarsus) became a follower of Jesus after a similar experience.

These facts are supported by multiple independent sources, including New Testament writings, early creeds, and extra-biblical historical records. 


## Significance and Application

The Minimal Facts approach allows scholars and apologists to construct a historical argument for the resurrection without relying on the full reliability of the New Testament or theological assumptions. By focusing on facts that even skeptical scholars accept, it provides a neutral starting point for evaluating competing explanations, including naturalistic hypotheses. The method also emphasizes inference to the best explanation, arguing that the resurrection hypothesis best accounts for the combination of these minimal facts. 

In summary, Habermas's Minimal Facts approach is a rigorous, historically grounded method that identifies a small set of widely accepted events surrounding Jesus' death and resurrection, forming a foundation for historical and apologetic analysis.
